Frequently Asked Questions

Where are you located for in-person lessons?

Institute is located at a private residence in South Windsor near South Windsor High School. Address to be shared once student enrolls in a program.

What subjects are taught at 180Math?

At 180Math, we offer structured math instruction from Grade 3 through high school, including AP Pre-Calculus, building strong foundations and advanced problem-solving skills.

What's the Cohort size?

For the 2026–27 academic year, each cohort will be limited to a small, single-digit number of students, fostering a highly focused and engaging learning environment. This intimate setting ensures personalized support for every learner while encouraging active participation (even from quieter students) so all can build confidence, contribute meaningfully, and collaborate effectively.

Do you offer homework help?

Challenging questions are always encouraged in class. They ignite deeper discussions and benefit everyone. That said, students are expected to make a genuine attempt at their homework before seeking help. Even incorrect or incomplete work is valuable, as it highlights exactly where guidance is needed and allows focused coaching.

Do you assign homework?

Yes, homework is assigned weekly through a combination of textbook problems and DeltaMath practice. We emphasize quality over quantity, focusing on thoughtfully selected, challenging questions rather than a high volume of work. Students are also expected to regularly review multiplication tables from 1 through 20 to build speed, accuracy, and confidence in mental math. To reinforce this, they will be quizzed frequently on their tables to develop quick and reliable recall. No matter what we do here at 180 Math, parental support will play an important role. Parents are asked to ensure the assignments are completed on time and with care. Students should maintain a dedicated notebook for homework to stay organized and track their progress.

When are the fees due?

Fees are expected to be paid by 5th of every month.

Will fees be prorated for the month of June since it has only two working weeks?

Yes. The June fees will be prorated, and the adjustment will be applied in April 2027, based on the official last day of the school year.

Are the fees prorated if my child is absent for a session?

No, fees are not prorated for student absences, as the class is still conducted for the rest of the group. If a student misses a session, the teacher will provide all materials covered—such as whiteboard notes, topics reviewed, and instructional videos. The student will also receive additional support in the following class to help them catch up.

Are the fees prorated if the teacher cancels a session?

Standard monthly fees are calculated with the understanding that up to two emergency teacher cancellations may occur during the academic year. If additional cancellations happen for any reason, every effort will be made to schedule a makeup class. If a makeup session cannot be arranged, the fees will be prorated in the following month.

The school calendar shows 42–43 weeks. Why are students receiving 36 weeks of lessons?

The school year includes 42 academic weeks, but 4 holiday weeks*** and 2 emergency teacher-cancellation weeks are already accounted for in the annual schedule. After excluding these six weeks, students receive 36 weeks of instruction.

If the school board extends the academic year by an additional week, we will be notified around April 2027, and class schedules will be extended accordingly.

Other than the 4 weeks mentioned, do students get additional holidays?

Currently, we follow the South Windsor Public School calendar. For Holidays falling on a weekday, such as Diwali, Holi, Presidents Day etc., parents will be consulted if a Holiday is needed. We will go with the majority. Fees will be prorated in the following month if the class is cancelled on a holiday.

– * Extra lessons are charged at Private lesson rate.

– ** No classes on during the Thanksgiving week, Christmas Holidays and Spring break.
